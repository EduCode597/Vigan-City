About Vigan

Vigan City, the pride of Ilocos Sur, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the New 7 Wonders Cities of the World known for its timeless blend of Spanish colonial charm and Filipino culture. Visitors can stroll along Calle Crisologo, lined with cobblestone streets, ancestral houses, and kalesas, and enjoy local delicacies like longganisa, empanada, and bagnet. Highlights include St. Paul’s Cathedral, Plaza Salcedo, Bantay Bell Tower, and the Syquia Mansion Museum. By night, the city glows with lights and music, especially during the dancing fountain show, offering travelers a perfect mix of history, culture, and romance.

Culture & Cuisine

Vigan’s culture is a harmonious blend of history, artistry, and tradition. Rooted in its Ilocano heritage, the city preserves age-old customs through its architecture, crafts, and community life. From the cobblestone streets and Spanish colonial houses to the vibrant festivals and local craftsmanship, Vigan stands as a living museum that celebrates both the beauty of the past and the spirit of its people.

From handwoven *abel* fabrics to savory *Vigan longganisa*, the city captures Ilocano artistry at its finest. Each craft and dish is a piece of tradition passed through generations. From its time-honored crafts to its distinct local flavors, Vigan reflects a culture deeply rooted in tradition and creativity. Every creation and recipe tells a quiet story of heritage, shaped by generations of skill and pride.

Plan Your Visit

Best Time to Visit

Cooler months (Nov–Feb) with festivals and cozy weather.

Getting There

8-hour bus ride from Manila or fly via Laoag Airport, then 2 hours by land.

Where to Stay

Charming heritage inns and boutique hotels along Calle Crisologo.
